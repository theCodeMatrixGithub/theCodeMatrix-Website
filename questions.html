Curiosity never actually killed the cat. We know you are curious. ​Here are the answers to your questions.

Q: What age group is theCodeMatrix for?
+ A: theCodeMatrix is for kids ages 8-16. Our programs are usually tailored towards specific age groups. You can check out specific age groups for our summer coding clubs for Summer 2018 on the Register tab. If you don't see a club that fits your needs, you can Start a Club or Request a Club by filling out one of the two forms on the Register tab.

Q: Do students need to have prior coding experience? ​
+ A: Nope! theCodeMatrix is for kids who are either interested in coding, have experience with coding, or may not have heard anything about it before. Our summer coding clubs and workshops are structured to give kids an exposure to what computer science is, where it can be applied, and how to write fundamental code. Our main goal is to teach kids to "think like programmers," and this is something anyone can do!

Q: How much does the program cost? ​
+ A: Nothing! It's absolutely free! We are a nonprofit club and all proceedings go to the club activities and any remaining amount will be saved for future programs. ​If you would like to donate to help us expand our efforts, please go to the Donate tab to access our PayPal.

Q: How do I sign up? ​
+ A: You can go to our Register page to sign up for our summer coding clubs. If you don't see a club in your city, fill out the form to Start a Club​.

Q: Are you looking for volunteers to run the program? ​
+ A: We are looking for teens entering grades 9-12 to run branches during the summer and monthly workshops during the school year. We are also looking for middle school students (grades 7-8) to become "interns." Interns can eventually become volunteers when they graduate middle school. If you are interested, apply on our Volunteer page and don't hesitate to email thecodematrix@gmail.com for more information. If you are part of another student run organization and would like to partner with theCodeMatrix, then email us! ​

Q: Is your club tailored to a specific gender? ​
+ A: Nope! We believe that kids of all genders, races, and socioeconomic backgrounds deserve a great education. We are striving to help bridge the gaps in computer science, specifically in STEM and business, through our projects. We also try to maintain a gender and age balance in our clubs and other programs.

Q: Are you looking for volunteers to run the program? ​
+ A: We have 7 branches in Summer 2018. If your elementary or middle school is interested in having a workshop on campus during the school year, email us! We are open to suggestions for libraries and schools that we may be able to hold programs in. Tell us more about your idea by emailing thecodematrix@gmail.com or fill out a form to Start a Branch by going to Register.

Q: You are planning to teach HTML/CSS, Java, and other complex aspects of coding and computer science to elementary school kids. Do you think they are ready to grasp this kind of knowledge? ​
+ A: Absolutely! Our approach to teaching these coding skills combines lessons and projects with fun and games. We strive to simplify the complex concepts and intertwine them with interactive activities that make coding fun for kids. Also, some programs that we use (Hour of Code and Scratch) are specifically designed for introducing kids to coding. Atom, the text editor we will be using for coding a static webpage, is also user-friendly due to color coding and clean user interface. Our Media team works with our Technical team (creators of the lesson plans) to make every club meeting lively, stimulating, and simply awesome. ​
